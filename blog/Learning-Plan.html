What is your long term goal or career pathway?

My long term goal is to become self-sufficient and employable with the ability to travel and follow my own interests outside of work.

How my culture has influenced my values and identity.

My Father is a New Zealand pakeha, my mother is Maltese. I was raised in Poneke, Wellington. My mother was raised with very little and as such has a culture that has very much raised me to be frugal and resourceful, with a great regard for the value of things and an aversion to waste. Furthermore family and community has always been an important part of my upbringing and remain important to me now.

My strengths and limitations

in terms of learning and career development, and their intersection with learning obstacles. I think one of my great strengths is in working as a team. I very much feed off of group energy and love to help others work together. As well as the feeling of encouragement that I get from contributing to a team. This I think will help me in my learning journey at DevAcademy in particular. This also intersects with a learning obstacle in that I find it hard to focus on my own work when I don’t have external forces keeping me focused.

An experience working with others where there was resistance or tension.

When I was working at Kiwibank as a software tester intern I had a group project with another intern. The intern that I was working with had completed her full Computer-Science degree while I had only done one year of mine before withdrawing. This project was analysis based and required research and the writing of a process framework for presentation. I found that my coworker was somewhat dismissive of my ideas and was very much trying to take the lead and somewhat do the project herself. I tried my best to facilitate our working together and work on our different parts then integrate them into the document together. This was somewhat successful but I could still feel that she didn’t really respect my contributions. I just tried my best to maintain a calm and productive environment. In the end she began to value my contributions and we delivered a project that we were both happy with. At some point following this she actually brought up to me that she was sorry for how she was acting and that she held a bit of resentment because she thought she would have seen me more at school if I had been there as we were in the same cohort (she was under the impression that I had said that I did the whole course). I explained to her that I had withdrawn for health reasons and she was very understanding and apologetic for her misconception. I think this was the best strategy I could have tried at the time. I could maybe try to clear up such an issue in the future by trying to ask the person directly, but I would make sure to be careful in my wording as I would not want to come off confrontational. I think the best strategy going forward would be to remain professional but bring up communication issues if they are there without being presumptuous or judgemental.
