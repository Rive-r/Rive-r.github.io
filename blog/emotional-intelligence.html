What is Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional intelligence is a set of sckills related to understanding yourself, and the people around you. And the ability to integrate this information into your thinking and actions.

How is EQ different to IQ?

IQ is a measure of ones ability to problem solve, use critical thinking and perform arithmetic tasks. It is derived from a set of standardised tests meant to rank a persons age against a standardised performance one might be expected to have at a certain age. Emotional intelligence is different in that it relates to how well you can empathise and understand what is happening within yourself and in those around you. This can vary greatly based on ones experience and outlook.

Why is it important?

Emotional intelligence is important for many reasons. Developing your EQ can lead to increased cognitive skills, a greater sense of calm or peace, and importantly how you interact and integrate with those around you. As humans are social creatures the benefits of gaining an increased understanding for yourself and those around you cannot be understated. It is also said that in the workplace having a good EQ is perhaps more important to career development than iq.
